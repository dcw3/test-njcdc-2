Carbon Dividend Calculator

Carbon fee and dividend puts a price on carbon and returns the money to households and businesses

About

The proposed policy puts a fee of $30 per metric ton of carbon dioxide at the point of entry to New Jersey.

For example, 10% ethanol gasoline outputs up to 17.6 pounds of carbon dioxide per gallon, so the fee on a gallon of gas is 17.6 pounds CO2 * (1 metric ton / 2205 pounds) * ($30 / 1 metric ton) = $0.24

This fee is payed by energy producers and sellers. For example, companies like ExxonMobil or BP would pay the fee as gasoline is shipped into New Jersey.

While ordinary citizens won't directly pay the fee, companies almost surely will have to increase the price of affected products. In our calculator, we make the conservative assumption that the entire burden of the fee gets passed along to consumers.


70% of the fee's revenue will be returned equally to all NJ citizens in the form of a check. Another 27% will go to subsidizing vulnerable businesses who, for example, face competition outside of NJ which won't be affected by this policy. The final 3% will go to administrative costs.
